Grade Point Averages (GPA)

What is a Grade Point Average?

A Grade Point Average (GPA) is used to assess success in your studies and to calculate admission rankings for applications to University.

A grade point average is the sum of the numerical grades for each course you have undertaken within the program you are studying, divided by the number of courses you have attempted.

What is GPA used for?

GPA is used to determine your success in your studies at Griffith College.

For example, if you maintain a GPA â‰¥ 3.5 and you have met other requirements outlined the Program Progression Policy, your academic performance is considered satisfactory.

If your GPA falls below a 3.5, you will be contacted by the College to discuss support strategies in the first instance.

Students progressing to some Griffith University bachelor degree programs will be required to satisfy GPA entry requirements as outlined in the Program Fact Sheets section of the portal.

What grades are counted when calculating your GPA?

All grades obtained in a specific program (Certificate IV in University Foundation Studies, or Diploma, or Associate Degree) are counted. This includes grades of Marginal Fail (F3), Fail (F2), Low Fail (F1), Fail Assessment Not Submitted (FS) and Withdrawn with Failure (WF).

For example, if you completed a course and failed, and then subsequently did the course again and passed, both your fail and pass grades will be included in your GPA calculation.


Calculating your GPA

To calculate your GPA you will need to follow the steps listed below:

  1. Add the Numeric Value for each course for which you have been awarded a final grade, to get a total numeric value (including failed courses);
  2. Count the Total Number of Courses for which you have been awarded a final grade (including failed courses);
  3. Divide the Total Numeric Value by the Total Number of Courses;
  4. Round the result to two decimal places to arrive at your GPA!
